# UK Property Finance Glossary

Shared reference loaded by skills that need consistent vocabulary. The definitions here are the RockCap-internal canon for terms a prospect, lender, or new joiner might use slightly differently elsewhere. Skills cite this glossary when their output uses any of these terms.

## Core financial terms

**GDV** (Gross Development Value): the expected value of the completed development at the point of sale or refinance. Used as the denominator in LTGDV. Skills should state the GDV's basis (red-book valuation, comparable evidence, sponsor estimate) when citing one.

**TDC** (Total Development Cost): land plus build cost plus professional fees plus finance costs plus contingency. The fully loaded cost of completing the development. Used as the denominator in LTC.

**LTGDV** (Loan to Gross Development Value): facility size divided by GDV. The senior development finance metric of choice. Typical UK ranges: 55-65% for prime schemes, 70% as a stretch, anything above 70% requires a strong sponsor or strong asset.

**LTC** (Loan to Cost): facility size divided by TDC. Often more useful than LTGDV during construction because it answers "how much of the build are we funding". Typical ranges: 70-85%.

**LTV** (Loan to Value): for investment or term loans, facility size divided by current market value of the asset. Different convention to LTGDV; never mix them. Typical UK ranges: 55-70% senior, up to 75% with strong covenant.

**Day-one value**: the value the lender ascribes on day one of the facility. For development finance, often equal to the purchase price plus signed agreements; for investment loans, the most recent valuation.

**Day-one release**: the cash the lender releases at facility drawdown, before any construction milestones. Land acquisition plus the early-stage professional fees, typically.

**Margin**: the lender's interest rate above the reference rate (SONIA or BBR). Quoted as basis points. Development finance margins in the current market sit typically between 600bps and 900bps; investment loans between 200bps and 400bps; bridging between 7% and 12% all-in.

**All-in rate**: the headline coupon, reference plus margin. The number a client cares about. Always state whether all-in is based on current reference or projected.

**Profit on cost** (PoC): scheme profit divided by TDC. A scheme below 15% PoC is hard to finance with senior development debt; below 10% needs equity-style structuring.

**Profit on GDV** (PoGDV): scheme profit divided by GDV. The cousin of PoC; lenders look at both.

**Coverage**: the ratio of GDV to facility plus interest. Should be above ~1.5x for the lender to be comfortable; tightens as LTGDV climbs.

## Products and structures

**Bridging finance / bridge loan**: short-tenor (typically under 24 months), high-margin, interest-only or retained-interest. Used for fast-execution purchases, refinances pending sale, or pre-development capital while planning consents are obtained. We use "bridging" by default; "bridge loan" specifically when the structure has the bridge-to-something feature explicit.

**Development finance / construction loan**: senior debt for the construction phase, sized against TDC with draws against construction milestones. Tenor typically 18-36 months. Monitored by a building surveyor on behalf of the lender (the QS or BCM).

**Investment loan / term loan**: longer-tenor debt against an income-producing asset (stabilised commercial, completed BTR, etc.). Margin lower, structure more covenant-heavy.

**Mezzanine**: subordinated debt sitting behind senior, ahead of equity. Usually expensive (15-25% all-in or with equity kicker). Used to stretch leverage when senior leaves a gap.

**PCP** (Profit Common Participation): structure where a senior or mezz lender takes a share of scheme profit alongside the coupon. Lender-friendly; sponsor-unfriendly unless very stretched.

**Preferred equity / equity layer**: capital senior to common equity but with a profit share. Often used where pure debt cannot stretch and the sponsor wants to dilute selectively.

**Stretch senior**: senior debt going to higher LTV/LTC than a conventional senior lender would (typically with a specialist lender). Sits between conventional senior and mezz in pricing and structure.

**Whole loan**: senior plus mezz from a single lender. Avoids inter-creditor friction; usually slightly more expensive than splitting.

**Refinance**: replacing existing debt with new debt, usually on better terms or to extract equity.

**Recapitalisation**: changing the equity stack, often alongside a refinance.

## Borrower-side terminology

**SPV** (Special Purpose Vehicle): a single-purpose company set up to own one scheme or one asset. Standard structure for UK property development. Lenders lend to the SPV; the parent group usually guarantees.

**Sponsor**: the principal or principals behind the borrower group. Lenders underwrite the sponsor as much as the deal.

**HoldCo / ParentCo / OpCo**: holding company, parent company, operating company. Often distinct entities; charges sit at different layers.

**Equity contribution**: the cash the sponsor puts into the scheme alongside debt. Typically 10-30% of TDC for development finance, more for higher-risk schemes.

**Profit share**: the sponsor's economics after debt is repaid and minimum returns are met.

## Lender-side terminology

**BDM** (Business Development Manager): the lender-side relationship manager who originates and underwrites deals. The person we talk to first at any lender. BDMs move between lenders frequently; tracking BDM mobility is part of lender intelligence.

**Credit Committee** / **IC** (Investment Committee): the lender-side approval body. Typically meets weekly or fortnightly. Deals move from "indicative terms" to "submitted for credit" when packs are sent for committee.

**Security agent**: the legal entity that holds security on behalf of a lender or syndicate. Apex, GLAS, US Bank Trustees, BNY Mellon are common UK security agents. When a security agent is on charge filings, the actual lenders are not publicly disclosed; this signals syndicate or fund-style lending.

**Indicative terms** / **heads of terms** / **term sheet**: the lender's offer document. Indicative is non-binding ("subject to credit"); a signed term sheet typically commits both sides to proceed unless conditions precedent fail.

**Credit-approved**: the lender has cleared the deal through their internal approval process. Strong commitment but still subject to legal completion.

**Conditions precedent** (CPs): the conditions a borrower must satisfy before drawdown. Planning consent, valuation, fully-signed building contract, equity deposit confirmed, etc.

**Drawdown**: the act of receiving funds. Development loans draw in tranches; bridging and term loans usually draw in one go.

## Planning and regulatory

**Planning consent / planning approval**: local authority approval for a scheme. The trigger event for many of our reachouts.

**S106**: section 106 agreement; the planning obligation under which a developer pays the local authority or delivers community benefit (affordable housing, infrastructure).

**CIL** (Community Infrastructure Levy): the statutory charge on most new development. Calculated per square metre, payable to the local authority.

**RP** (Registered Provider): housing association or for-profit RP. Buyers of affordable housing under S106 agreements.

**RICS Red Book**: the valuation standards UK property valuations follow. A "Red Book valuation" is the formal valuation lenders require for facility decisioning.

**BCM** / **MS** / **QS**: monitoring surveyor variants. The independent professional who certifies construction progress and signs off draw releases on behalf of the lender.

## Scheme types

**Residential development**: build for private sale or build-to-rent.

**Mixed-use**: residential plus commercial (typically ground-floor retail or office).

**Student accommodation** / **PBSA** (Purpose-Built Student Accommodation).

**BTR** (Build to Rent): residential built for the rental market, sold to a single institutional buyer.

**Commercial development**: offices, industrial, logistics, retail. Currently the office segment in particular is under structural pressure.

**Hotel / Operating asset**: schemes with an operating component; valued partly on income from operations.

**PRS** (Private Rented Sector): the broader landlord market, distinct from BTR's institutional shape.

## Numbers and conventions

- All money values are GBP unless stated.
- Property areas in square feet (commercial) or square metres (residential, planning).
- Dates in ISO format (YYYY-MM-DD) when stored; in DD Month YYYY when written in prose.
- Percentages always include the % sign in prose. "70%" not "0.7".
- Ranges use "to" not en-dashes ("70% to 75% LTGDV", not "70-75%").
