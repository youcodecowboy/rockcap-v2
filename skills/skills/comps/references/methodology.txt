## 2A. COMP DISCOVERY (DO THIS FIRST, BEFORE BUILDING ANY TIERS)

Added after the Thornton Heights (WVBC1) miss, June 2026. Our pipeline (PropertyData + Gemini + Claude) missed the single best comp in the deal (Flat 5, 101 Ridgway, GBP 1,137 psf) plus three same-street Thornton Road sales, while Alex found them in a one-minute Rightmove sold-price search. Root cause: we optimised for automated breadth and skipped the simplest, highest-yield step. These rules exist so it does not happen again. Discovery is comps-first, frame-second: build and verify the comp universe BEFORE constructing tiers. Never set up the appraisal-driven tier structure and then search to fill it, that biases discovery toward confirming the pricing thesis.

### Step 1 (mandatory): browse the subject street and adjacent streets by hand
Before any API or Gemini work, open the Rightmove house-prices sold list (and Zoopla) for the subject postcode and EVERY immediately adjacent street and postcode. Capture every sale of every type for the last 18 to 24 months. The subject street is the highest-value comp source in any deal and must be exhausted manually first. For a tight prime micro-market a human scrolling the street sold-list out-performs the whole API pipeline, because the APIs' coverage gaps are invisible. The simple Rightmove browse is not optional and not beneath the tooling, it is the first and highest-yield step.

### Step 2: discover exhaustively, curate later
Pull ALL sales by location and type first. Do NOT pre-filter discovery by the pricing thesis or by "new-build / prime only". Houses are valid comps for apartments (same-street location evidence), and high-value period flats / maisonettes are valid even when they are not "new-build conversions". Tier and curate for relevance AFTER the universe is complete, never before.

### Step 3: treat every API as a SAMPLE, never the market
PropertyData and similar feeds have systematic blind spots. `sold-prices-per-sqf` only returns properties that carry an EPC-derived sqft and it caps results (about 20 rows), so maisonettes, large laterals and high-value units that lack a clean EPC area are silently excluded. Worked proof: for 101 Ridgway the endpoint returned Flat 3 (GBP 470k) and Flat 4 (GBP 535k), the two cheapest, but NOT Flat 5 (GBP 1.21m), the best comp in the same building, so the "secondhand tops out at GBP 747 psf" read was an artifact of the filtered sample. Mandatory counter-query: always run a "highest-price in radius" AND a "highest GBP/sqf in radius" pull sorted descending, to catch the top end the per-sqf endpoint drops. NEVER state "the market tops out at GBP X" or "no evidence above GBP X" from a single API pull.

### Step 4: exclusion lists are TRANSACTION-LEVEL only
When telling Gemini or an agent what is already captured, exclude only specific (address + date + price) transactions. NEVER exclude a building, street or postcode. Excluding "101 Ridgway" as a building is exactly how we told the search to skip the building that held the best comp. Building, street and postcode-level exclusions are banned.

### Step 5: completeness gate before any conclusion or build
Before stating a psf range, drawing a "no evidence above X" conclusion, or building the schedule, confirm all three: (1) every same-street sale in the last 18 to 24 months is captured or consciously excluded with a reason; (2) the top 10 highest-GBP/sqf flats AND houses in radius are reviewed; (3) the top 10 highest-price sales in radius are reviewed. If any surfaces a comp not already in the schedule, discovery is not finished. Log what was searched and what was NOT covered (e.g. "PropertyData returned 20 of N flats, high-value tail absent from the per-sqf endpoint") so gaps are visible, not silent.

### 3.1 Appendix A � Master Comparable Schedule (Excel or Word table)
**Purpose:** The single source of truth for all comparable transactions. Every verified comp goes here. Investment committee members click through the evidence links to verify claims.

**Structure:**
- Organised into tiers (e.g. Tier 1: Developer Direct Comp, Tier 2: Peer Village NB, Tier 3: Subject Village Prime Resale, Tier 4: Inferior Stock Floor, Tier 5: Peer Village Resale)
- Tier headers show psf range and role (e.g. "Subject village value ceiling")
- 10 columns, canonical order (per Dark Mills): Address/Unit | Scheme | Date | Price (�) | SqFt | �/psf | Type | Beds | Notes | Evidence Link. Houses-led schedules use **Beds**. For apartment / gated-estate schedules where service charge matters, swap the Beds column for **Svc Chg** (as in the AL Houses master). Never carry both; pick Beds or Svc Chg to fit the scheme.
- Column J (Evidence) = clickable hyperlink. Display text = the property address / unit name (e.g. "Sperrings, Witney Lane", "5 Hicks Close"). Target = the source URL (RM UUID detail page preferred).
- No separate raw-URL column. The hyperlinked cell serves both purposes � display for quick identification, target for click-through.
- �/psf column uses a PLAIN division: `=D{row}/E{row}`. Do NOT wrap it in IF/AND guards (Rayn's standing rule, reconfirmed June 2026: all formulas simple multiplication/division). If a row has no sqft yet (TBC / n/a), leave the �/psf cell BLANK and drop in the simple `=D/E` once the sqft is in. Never copy the over-engineered IF formulas seen in some older example files.
- After any save, run `recalc.py` to recalculate cached formula values

**Notes column (Col I) discipline:**
- Keep to one line � "why this is a comparable" only.
- Must include (a) scheme/stock descriptor, (b) size-match to a specific subject plot, (c) tier role in one phrase.
- Example format: "Prime Leafield detached character. Size-matched to Plots 4 & 5; establishes village prime-resale ceiling."
- **Always relate the comp back to the specific subject unit in the accommodation schedule, and say how it supports our GDV.** The note's job is to tell the lender why this transaction justifies the subject unit's price, via the delta (size, condition, spec, age, typology). Pattern: "[comp] is [smaller / older / needs work / inferior spec] than the subject [unit], so the subject's [�X / �Y psf] is justified / conservative." E.g. "Dated 2-bed resale, c.120 sqft smaller than the subject NB 2-bed, so the subject's psf premium is supported." Read the Example Comps files for this unit-by-unit tie-back style (especially the Horton anchor-per-type layout). Every comp must earn its place by reference to a subject plot, not float as generic market colour. NB: the older AL Houses master uses terser source-only notes (e.g. "Elivia Stourton"); the subject-unit tie-back is the standard going forward ? do not regress to source-only notes.
- Do NOT clutter Notes with verification commentary, sqft-source notes, EPC caveats, garage flags, or �/psf arithmetic. That belongs in the project working notes or the Forensic Report, not the Appendix.
- **Counter-argument preemption pattern.** When a comp invites an obvious objection (typology mismatch, dated stock, atypical layout, bungalow psf inflation), use the preemption pattern: `[main observation]. [obvious counter-argument acknowledged]. [why the comp still works]`. Example: "�505 psf for a 2019 modern 2-bed bungalow (EPC B). Bungalow typology will drive a single-floor premium, however the �/sqft is good evidence at this size band." Strict one-line discipline can extend to two sentences only when preempting a counter-argument.

**Curation over coverage (Alex's tier discipline):**
- Tier count is editorial, not exhaustive. The schedule serves the narrative, not breadth.
- When a tier sits more than 2 miles from the subject and its psf doesn't actively support the narrative (i.e. low psf without useful framing, or distant NB that just dilutes the picture), cut it.
- Lender attention is the binding constraint. A 60-row schedule lender skims is worse than a 25-row schedule lender reads.
- Worked example: Dark Mills V1_4 had 5 tiers and 41 rows including Rooksmoor Mills (1.8mi tired-stock NB), Church Square (2.2mi premium NB) and Lansdown Rise (2.5mi contemporary NB). All three got stripped in review, leaving Lewiston Mill (the 500-ft direct comp) plus the local resale baseline plus a pending over-�500k section.

**Build in two passes (don't polish too early):**
- **First pass:** get the verified comps in ? address, price, date, sqft (or TBC), type, link, and a brief note. LEAVE tier headers, psf-range headers, and polished conventions BLANK or light. These churn constantly while sqft/floor plans are still being verified, so finalising them now is wasted work (Rayn, June 2026).
- **Final first-draft pass (after Rayn has verified floor plans / sqft):** add tier headers with psf ranges and role, finalise the subject-unit tie-back notes, renumber tiers, and polish formatting. Header/convention rules (tier-header format, ASKING placement, date style) are a FINAL-pass concern, not a first-round one.

**Excel technical rules (CRITICAL � learned from painful experience):**
- **openpyxl row insertion breaks hyperlink mapping.** NEVER insert rows into an existing Excel file with hyperlinks. Always rebuild the entire workbook from a complete master data array. This is non-negotiable.

## 5. DATA RULES

### 5.1 PSF Formula
**Price � SqFt.** That's it. Never overcomplicate. Never estimate � always calculate. Show the maths if asked.

### 5.2 Sqft hierarchy (priority order)
1. **Rightmove floor plan** from specific property detail page (check each unit's page individually)
2. **Developer brochure / specification sheet** � published sqft per house type (confirm "Total Internal Living Area" = habitable GIA excluding garages)
3. **Agent brochure / listing** � Zoopla, OnTheMarket, or agent website
4. **Planning application GIA schedule** � approved docs on council portal
5. **Bricksandlogic / StreetScan** � EPC-derived postcode average (state source)
6. **Chimnie** � EPC fallback. Lower confidence. Frequently understates sqft for NB vs developer brochures. Treat as approximate.
7. **No sqft found** � do not guess a number, but do NOT discard an otherwise strong comp just because its sqft is missing or wasn't found on the first research round. Carry it with sqft "TBC" (or "n/a" where LR never published it), leave the �/psf blank, and keep trying across rounds (your own WebFetch, floor plans, Bricksandlogic, Rayn). Only drop it after genuine repeated failure and a manual call. A good comp is never thrown out for a first-round miss (Rayn, June 2026). NB: this is about missing SQFT only ? the SALE itself must still be verified (see 6.1).

**Never use a generic EPC figure when a specific unit's Rightmove listing has a floor plan.**

### 5.3 Price types
- **Achieved prices only** unless explicitly labelled. Land Registry confirmed via Rightmove house-prices, Zoopla, or Bricksandlogic.
- **Asking prices** must be clearly labelled "ASKING" in the Notes column and excluded from averaged �/psf calculations.
- **Marketing prices** from developer websites are asking prices � label accordingly.
- If a developer has withdrawn asking prices (e.g. Osprey), note this.

### 5.4 Link standards
- **Specific Rightmove detail page UUID links ONLY:** `rightmove.co.uk/house-prices/details/[UUID]`
- **Never link a comp's Evidence cell to the area/postcode page** (`rightmove.co.uk/house-prices/[postcode].html`) ? it is NOT investment committee standard.
- **Claude fetches the specific UUID itself. Do NOT hand Rayn a generic link, and do NOT say "detail page to be fetched / verify manually" as a substitute for fetching it.** The detail-page UUID is reachable: WebFetch the Rightmove house-prices postcode/street page (e.g. `house-prices/hr7-4dq.html`), which lists every sale on that page together with its per-transaction detail-page link, and read the specific `details/[UUID]` URL for each comp straight off it. Do this as a matter of course, exactly like pulling PropertyData transaction URLs. Confirmed working on Linton Lane (June 2026): a single fetch of the postcode page returned the exact `details/[UUID]` for every same-street sale.
- Only after a genuine fetch attempt fails (page dead, or the comp is not listed) fall back to a Zoopla/OTM detail page; flag "detail page not located" only as a last resort once you have actually tried.
- **Hyperlink display text = a short identifying label, enough to know which property it is** ? e.g. "RM 8 Oakfields", "S&P Hoo Park", or the unit name / short address. It does NOT need the full address, but it must NOT be a bare "RM Link" / "RM Detail" (too little to identify the comp). Confirmed by Rayn, June 2026. The cell shows this label and links to the source URL.
- Rayn still does a final click-test on fetched UUIDs as light QA, but the default is that Claude has already populated the live, specific link.

### 5.5 Agent data verification
**Agent schedules require independent verification.** Previous project confirmed at least one Carter Jonas plot-to-type mapping error (Plot 7 Oakfields � CJ assigned 5-bed Charlton at 2,584 sqft; Rightmove floor plan showed 4-bed Spencer at 2,990 sqft, which initially appeared to disprove CJ, but developer brochure later confirmed CJ was right and the RM floor plan included the garage). - RM floor plans may include garages � check whether the stated sqft says "excluding garage" or not

- Never rely on a single source for sqft

### 5.6 Calculated averages
- When the Appendix tier header states an average (e.g. "�612 psf"), verify it by calculating from the individual transactions. If the header says �612 but the calculation yields �604, use the calculated figure.
- Blended averages across a tier = total value of all transactions � total sqft of all transactions. NOT the average of individual �/psf figures (which weights small and large units equally).

### 5.7 Distance framing
- When the distance can be expressed in more than one accurate unit, quote the more compelling figure.
- 500 ft beats 0.4 mile when the comp is on the same road. 1.8 mile beats 2.9 km even though both are correct.
- Rule: convert distance to whichever unit produces the smallest defensible number, then state it in the tier header.
- Never round in the misleading direction (do not call 0.45 mile "500 ft"). The number must still be true.

### 5.8 Proximity columns (Distance + Drive time) - OPTIONAL, automated
Some schedules carry two extra columns after Evidence: Distance (straight-line mi from the scheme site to each comp) and Drive (Google Maps, min). NOT standard on every appendix - add only when a scheme wants proximity evidence (origin: Alex's Oakridge Lynch call-note, June 2026). They make the proximity-vs-price tension explicit, which is exactly what a valuer probes when the strong psf comps sit further out.
- **Populate via the skill helper:** `scripts/drive_times.py` (in this skill). `populate_appendix(path, origin="<scheme postcode>")` fills both columns. Distance = postcodes.io geocode + haversine (exact, no key). Drive time = Google Routes API computeRouteMatrix (Google's own engine, so figures match the app).
- **Drive time MUST come from Google Routes, not OSRM.** The free no-key OSRM demo server routes on posted speed limits with no traffic and runs ~20-40% short of Google on rural lanes - it produced wrong (optimistic) Oakridge times that Rayn caught against Google. - **Key:** needs `GOOGLE_MAPS_API_KEY` in `~/.claude/.env` (Maps Platform key, Routes API enabled, on the firm GCP project). Inside Google's monthly free tier at our comp volumes. If the key is absent, the helper errors - either add it or fall back to manual: generate one `google.com/maps/dir/?api=1&origin=..&destination=..` link per comp for Rayn to click and read off.
- Subject units = 0 mi / "On site". Village comps sharing the scheme outcode resolve near 0 - if a comp lacks a full postcode the distance is village-level only, flag it (don't print a false 0.0).

## 6. VERIFICATION DISCIPLINE

**These rules exist because previous deliverables had errors that cascaded.**

### 6.1 No hallucinating data
If you cannot verify a transaction (that the SALE happened, at that price/date) against a Rightmove detail page, Land Registry record, or other primary source, do not include it. Flag it as "Unverified" or "Data Gap."
Distinguish two things: an unverified SALE is excluded; a verified sale with only its SQFT or secondary detail missing is NOT excluded ? carry it with sqft TBC and keep chasing (see 5.2 item 7). Do not conflate "couldn't find the sqft / detail page first round" with "couldn't verify the sale" ? a good comp survives a first-round miss.

### 6.2 Stale references cascade
When ANY primary figure changes (a price, a sqft, a psf), search the ENTIRE document for every other place that figure or any derivative appears. Update all downstream references � key takeaways, summary tables, tier headers, conclusion paragraphs, exec summary � in the same pass. Not just the primary data cell.

### 6.3 Verify the output file, not the working copy
After building or editing any document, verify the fix against the actual file in `/mnt/user-data/outputs/` � not the working copy in `/home/claude`. Open, read, and confirm before telling Rayn it's done.

### 6.4 Batch fixes, then review
Rayn prefers to accumulate corrections before implementing, not fix-as-you-go. When reading through a document, keep a running numbered list of issues. Implement all fixes in one clean pass at the end.

### 6.5 Rayn handles minor edits
For small text changes (a word, a number, a note), provide the exact value/text for Rayn to paste in himself. Don't burn usage on trivial edits unless instructed.

### 6.6 Floor plan images
When Rayn provides floor plan images, calculate sqft directly from visible dimensions without waiting to be asked. Flag if the stated sqft includes/excludes garage.

### 6.7 Renumber tiers after removal
When tiers are stripped during review, renumber the remaining tiers sequentially before the next version goes to Alex or lender. A schedule with "Tier 1" followed by "Tier 5" (because Tiers 2-4 were cut) looks unfinished and undermines the document. Also re-check the exec summary, tier-summary tables, and Key Takeaways for stale tier references.

### 6.8 Pending sections as working markers
Inline TODO blocks labelled e.g. "to be saved in another tier" with candidate URLs are acceptable in interim versions for Rayn's working state. Before going to Alex or lender they must be (a) re-formatted into proper rows with correct column placement (URL in Evidence column J, not Address column A; price in D, sqft in E, etc.), (b) given a tier header above with role and psf range, (c) Notes properly worded per Notes column discipline. Working-state markers shipped raw will be flagged by Alex as draft and bounce back.

## 8. WORKFLOW

### Scope first, then plan, then build (heavy-use mode)
**Ask ONE scoping question before doing anything else (before Superpowers).** Comps jobs split into two builds and you must know which before planning:
- **(a) Simple appendix (default for small deals).** A small loan / small build (this Linton Lane deal is the canonical example): subject unit(s) at the top, a one-line finding / revised-GDV block if it is a GDV test, then a handful of curated comps in light tiers. No Forensic Report or Deep Dive unless asked. Most jobs are this.
- **(b) Full comp complex (large/complex schemes).** Horton is the gold-standard exemplar: hero-anchor-per-house-type layout, multiple tiers/tabs, a "why this comp supports our pricing" column, plus the Forensic Report and Deep Dive. Reserve for large multi-unit schemes that genuinely need it.

If the deal is obviously small, default to (a) and say so rather than asking. If genuinely unsure, ask the one question. THEN, for (b) or any non-trivial (a), invoke the Superpowers planning skills (`superpowers:brainstorming` to scope output/geography/segmentation, then `superpowers:writing-plans`) before producing anything. A quick single-comp check (Modes 2-3, 7-8) needs neither the question nor planning. Standing instruction from Rayn (June 2026).

### Research: Gemini ? Claude
- **Gemini Deep Research** handles web searches, transaction hunting, sqft verification
- **Claude** constructs the documents from Gemini's raw output
- This keeps Claude usage efficient and avoids exhausting quota on searches
- Rayn provides paste-ready search strings for Gemini

### When Gemini output arrives
1. **Verify every transaction** Gemini claims before putting it in any document. Gemini hallucinates links and prices.
2. **Cross-check sqft** against developer brochures / Bricksandlogic. Gemini sometimes uses asking-price sqft from current listings for sold properties.
3. **Where Gemini reports a DATA GAP, "could not verify", or "UUID not found", do NOT accept it at face value � fetch it yourself first.** Claude's WebFetch routinely reaches Rightmove/Zoopla house-price pages Gemini's browser cannot, so a Gemini gap is a prompt to verify, not a conclusion. WebFetch the relevant postcode/street page (and run PropertyData) and confirm the sale plus its detail-page UUID. Only record a DATA GAP after your own fetch also fails. Worked proof (Linton Lane, June 2026): Gemini flagged "Zillian" and "Garth Cottage" as unverifiable with no link; one WebFetch of each postcode page returned both as real LR sales with exact UUIDs.
4. **Flag what doesn't check out** rather than silently dropping it. Where Gemini's price is right but its sqft / type / beds are wrong (it routinely guesses these), correct against the primary source and keep the verified sale.

### Document construction
- Use Node.js `docx` library for Word output (read the SKILL.md first every time)
- Use openpyxl for Excel (always rebuild from master array, never insert rows)
- Run `recalc.py` after any Excel save with formulas
- Copy final outputs to `/mnt/user-data/outputs/` and present to Rayn

### 11.4 Gemini research workflow

**Step 1:** Claude drafts a Gemini Deep Research prompt with:
- Explicit list of properties already in the Appendix (DO NOT DUPLICATE), at TRANSACTION level only (address + date + price), NEVER a building / street / postcode (Section 2A Step 4)
- Specific postcodes and search instructions
- Expected output format (tables with: Address | Date | Price | SqFt | �/psf | Type | Source URL)
- Rules: achieved prices only, specific RM links, flag data gaps

**Step 2:** Rayn pastes into Gemini and runs the search.

**Step 3:** Rayn pastes Gemini's raw output back to Claude.

**Step 4:** Claude verifies every transaction against primary sources, **self-fetches anything Gemini marked as a gap or "can't verify" (Claude reaches pages Gemini cannot), self-fetches the specific RM detail-page UUIDs**, corrects Gemini's guessed sqft/type/beds against the source, flags only what survives its own fetch as a genuine gap, and constructs the document.

**Two-prompt refinement:** First pass often has gaps (missing villages, sqft not found, wrong property types). A follow-up Gemini prompt targets the specific gaps. This is more efficient than re-running the entire search.
